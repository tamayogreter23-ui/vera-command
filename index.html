The Greeks that matter daily

Direction, time, and volatility — in plain English.

Δ DeltaDirection. How much the option moves per $1 in the stock. ~0.50 at the money. Higher delta = more stock-like.
Θ ThetaTime decay. What you lose each day just from time passing. It works against buyers, for sellers. Worst near expiration.
V VegaVolatility. How much the option moves when implied volatility (IV) changes. High IV = expensive options = crush risk.
Γ GammaAcceleration. How fast delta changes. High near expiry — why short-dated options whip around.

Buy or sell premium? — decision tree

The single most useful options question, every day.

STEP 1 — IV
Is implied volatility (IV Rank) high or low right now?
LOW IV + directional view
Buy premium (long calls/puts or debit spreads). Options are cheap; you're paying little for time.
HIGH IV + directional view
Lean to debit spreads or selling premium (credit spreads) — don't overpay for rich vol, and dodge the IV crush.
EARNINGS this week?
Beware IV crush. Buying single options into earnings often loses even when you're right on direction. Spreads reduce it.
Education, not advice. IV Rank and the expected move are on your ThinkorSwim chain — Vera flags earnings/IV-crush timing in your morning brief.
